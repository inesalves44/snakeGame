SNAKE HISTORY

Game Play

The player must keep the snake from colliding with both other obstacles and itself, which gets harder as the snake lengthens.

History

The Snake game originated in 1976 with the arcade title Blockade, developed and published by Gremlin. It quickly gained popularity.

The game made its way to home computers with Worm, programmed by Peter Trefonas for the TRS-80 and published in CLOAD magazine in 1978. Trefonas went on to create versions for the Commodore PET and Apple II as well. The Snake Byte game was published in 1982 for various platforms, including Atari 8-bit computers, Apple II, and VIC-20. Snake Byte involved the snake eating apples to complete levels and growing longer as it progressed. The BBC Micro game Snake (1982) by Dave Bresnen featured a snake controlled using the left and right arrow keys, with the snake's speed increasing as it grew longer and only one life available. Nibbler (1982) was an arcade game that featured a fast-paced gameplay with the snake navigating a maze, and the Tron arcade game of the same year introduced a snake-like game mode called "light cycles," revitalizing the snake concept and inspiring subsequent games. In 1997 is arrived it's most iconic time with the Nokia phones.
